Clinical indications

Where SAGE
fits your practice

Indication 01

Psychedelic-assisted therapy
preparation & integration

Before any pharmacological intervention, SAGE familiarises patients with non-ordinary states — reducing adverse reactions, ego-dissolution anxiety, and treatment resistance. Patients arrive knowing their inner landscape. The music becomes a trusted container, not an unknown.

Post-session, the protocol extends and deepens therapeutic processing, significantly increasing the window of neuroplasticity following a psychedelic experience.

Indication 02

Standalone intervention
PTSD · Anxiety · Depression

For patients for whom psychedelic-assisted therapy is contraindicated, financially inaccessible, or personally undesired, SAGE provides comparable depth of access via acoustic induction alone.

The emotional release and grief protocols access stored somatic memory without requiring verbal articulation of traumatic content — particularly effective for complex PTSD and high alexithymia or dissociation.

Group & community
therapeutic settings

Individual psychedelic therapy costs £2,000–£10,000 per course. SAGE offers a clinically credible alternative at a fraction of the cost, with synchronised communal sessions allowing multiple patients to journey simultaneously and integrate in groups of up to ten.

Designed for community mental health clinics, residential treatment, and NHS group therapy settings.

Indication 04

Patients who cannot or will not
use psychedelics

Contraindications to psychedelic-assisted therapy — including personal history of psychosis, schizophrenia spectrum conditions, certain cardiovascular conditions, or patient preference — exclude a large population from depth-oriented altered-state work.

SAGE provides a safe, graded pathway for these populations, fully personalised and practitioner-guided at every point.

SAGE does not aim to maximise intensity. It aims to optimise safety, readiness, and integration.

The clinical protocol

End-to-end structured pathway —
intake through to programme report

Step 01

Patient baseline assessment

Validated intake using a resilience scale, prior altered-state experience scoring across five domains, and trait-level psychological profiling. Generates a clinical readiness score and recommended entry pathway.

Validated resilience scale + ASC scoring
Output: readiness tier + recommended protocol path
Step 02

Intention setting & plan generation

The client selects from five clinical intention pathways, or co-creates a specific therapeutic focus with the therapist. The system generates a personalised programme: session number, duration, frequency, timing, and set and setting guidance.

Pathways: Deep Rest · Expanded Awareness · Creative Flow · Emotional Processing · Clarity
Optional tasks for between-session holistic integration
Step 03

Session — induction to close

Mood assessment, somatic activation, a personalised music journey with optional voice recording, and reflective prompts. HRV monitoring via wearable if enabled. The sonic journey adapts in real time based on biofeedback.

Pre-session mood assessment + personalised journey + HRV data
Peak: adaptive music generated by biofeedback
Step 04

AI-guided integration — 5 dimensions

Post-session integration is one of the strongest predictors of intervention success. The AI guide conducts structured inquiry across somatic, visual, auditory, relational, and memory dimensions — cross-referencing in-session voice recordings.

Session fully recorded — accessible to patient and clinician
Individual or group integration (max 10 per circle)
Step 05 · Programme Close

Clinical report — outcomes & recommendations

At programme close, SAGE produces a comprehensive clinical report: HRV trajectory across sessions, peak moment mapping, language pattern analysis, resilience scale delta, and recommended continuation pathway. The report is presented to the clinician for review, editing, and approval before use.

HRV trajectory per session
Language pattern & intention alignment

Clinical measurement

Outcomes you can
document and defend

Resilience Scale

Trait change in coping mechanisms over time

The resilience scale is administered at intake, mid-programme, and close. Delta scores provide a clean, peer-reviewed outcome metric for resilience change — suitable for clinical records, insurance submissions, and research contribution.

Connor & Davidson, 2003 · Validated in 40+ languages

HRV

Heart Rate Variability Monitoring

Optional integration with consumer HRV wearables (Apple Watch, Garmin, Polar). The system reads autonomic state in real time and adapts integration questioning depth — a dysregulated nervous system receives containment; a regulated one receives depth inquiry.

Thayer et al., 2012 · Neuroscience & Biobehavioural Reviews

NLP

Language Pattern Analysis

Every word spoken during integration is analysed for semantic proximity to the stated therapeutic intention. The system tracks linguistic evolution across sessions — from avoidance to engagement, from abstraction to embodiment, from past to present.

Pennebaker et al., LIWC framework · Adapted for therapeutic use

Music & Sound Design

A curated and adaptive
sonic architecture

The SAGE protocol draws from multiple musical and acoustic approaches — each selected and composed based on its capacity to guide shifts in perception, emotion, and physiological state. Each journey is not a playlist, but a structured progression where sound acts as the primary guide.

What the music is designed to do

Modulate attention and focus
Access emotional and somatic memory
Facilitate non-verbal processing
Support safe entry into altered states
Guide safe return from altered states
Adapt in real time to physiological state

The sonic approaches

Ambient & Minimal Compositions

For nervous system regulation and grounding. These works create a stable acoustic container — the foundation from which deeper states become accessible.

Psychoacoustic Textures

To influence spatial perception and internal focus. Sound that operates below the level of conscious attention, shifting the relationship between self and environment.

Rhythmic Entrainment

To support state transitions and emotional processing. Tempo and rhythm as physiological tools — calibrated to the specific phase of the journey.

Harmonic & Tonal Structures

Designed to evoke tension, release, and resolution. Harmonic language selected for its capacity to move emotional material without requiring narrative.

Dynamic Soundscapes

That evolve in response to the listener's physiological state. Each journey is a structured progression — sound as the primary guide through different depths of experience.

Spatial & Immersive Sound

Drawing on electroacoustic composition and diffusion. The spatial dimension of sound is a therapeutic tool, dissolving the boundaries of ordinary perception.
